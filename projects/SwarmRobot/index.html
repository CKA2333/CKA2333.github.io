Abstract

We introduce an elastic-driven self-folding approach that fabricates robots directly from flat 3D-printed conductive PLA nets. Elastic bands routed through printed hooks store energy that folds the sheet into programmed 3D geometries, while the flat state allows accurate placement of electronics and magnets before deployment. The conductive PLA substrate simultaneously serves as structural material and electrode, enabling capacitive touch sensing through the robot's faces and a reusable snap-fit interface with Hall-effect sensors and vibration motors. We derive a closed-form model predicting equilibrium fold angles from hinge geometry and elastic band stiffness, and validate it across polyhedral variants. Three application demonstrations — modular swarm cubes, a deployable flat-printed gripper, and tendon-actuated robotic fingers — confirm the platform's versatility without requiring heat, specialized materials, or external stimuli.
